Finding


No difference between bulge-dominated galaxies in the clusters and the field.
Disky galaxies are more compact and more bulge-dominated in clusters. They are also fainter in clusters, implying lower mass.

Once the compact remnant is formed, its properties do not change with environment. Here we see the ongoing quenching of low-mass disky galaxies in clusters!

Warning: this analysis uses slightly older results, without robust Monte Carlo analysis.
